## PUBLIC TRANSPORT

Valencia has 6 metro lines, 3 tram lines, and ~60 bus routes. Most tourist areas are in **Zone A**.

### Best Ticket Options

| Ticket | Price | Best for |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **SUMA 10** (10 trips, Zone A) | ~8 EUR + 1 EUR card | Occasional rides. **Shareable** between two people. 1 free transfer per trip. |
| **SUMA T** (unlimited 1-3 days) | 4-12 EUR | Heavy transport days |
| **Valencia Tourist Card** (24/48/72h) | 15/20/25 EUR | Transport + free museums + discounts (see below) |

**Current deal:** 40% discount on SUMA cards until June 30, 2026.

### Valencia Tourist Card - Worth It?

**What's included:** Unlimited transport (incl. airport), free municipal museums, discounts on major attractions (up to 50% off), free tapa + drink.

**Best bundle:** 72h card + Oceanografic + Science Museum + Hemisferic = ~60 EUR/person (saves ~12 EUR vs. buying separately).

**For 7 days:** Consider one 72h bundle card for the days you visit City of Arts and Sciences, then SUMA 10 cards for the rest. Or just walk/bike - the city is very compact.

- https://www.visitvalencia.com/en/valencia-tourist-card

## CYCLING

Valencia is flat with 200+ km of bike lanes - one of Spain's best cycling cities.

### Valenbisi (City Bike Share)
- **Weekly pass:** 13.30 EUR
- **Trick:** First 30 min of every ride is free. Dock the bike before 30 min, take another one = unlimited free rides all week.
- Register at any Valenbisi terminal or valenbisi.es
- 2,750 bikes at 275 stations

### Private Rentals
- Standard bike: ~8 EUR/day
- E-bike: ~12-15 EUR/day
- Companies: Cobra Renta Bike, Valencia Bikes, Sky Bike Rent

### Rules
- Bike lanes are **mandatory** when they exist
- **No sidewalk riding** (fines!)
- **No headphones** while cycling
- Helmets required only for under 16
- Lights required at night

## FOOD & EATING

### Meal Costs (per person)

| Meal | Cost |
|------|------|
| Coffee + pastry (breakfast) | 2-4 EUR |
| **Menu del dia** (lunch, 3 courses + drink) | **10-18 EUR** |
| Tapas meal with drinks | 15-25 EUR |
| Paella at a restaurant | 15-22 EUR |
| Beer (cana) | 1.50-3 EUR |
| Glass of wine | 2-4 EUR |
| Budget meal (bocadillo, 100 Montaditos) | 3-8 EUR |

### Menu del Dia (Your Best Friend)
Weekday lunchtimes (13:00-15:30) most restaurants offer a **3-course set menu with drink** for 10-18 EUR. The same dishes a la carte would cost 2-3x more. Not usually available on weekends.

### Paella Rules
- Paella is a **LUNCH dish** - eating it at dinner screams tourist
- Traditional Valencian paella = rabbit, chicken, beans (not seafood)
- Good paella takes 20-30 min to cook. If it arrives in 5 min, it was premade.
- Avoid restaurants with paella photos on the menu outside
- **Sunday lunch is the traditional paella day**

### Best Areas for Eating
- **Ruzafa** - Trendy, creative tapas, best value. La Tasqueta del Mercat has set menus from 11.90 EUR
- **El Carmen** (Old Town) - Classic tapas bars. Bar La Pilareta is a century-old gem
- **Cabanyal** (Beach) - Authentic paella and seafood. Casa Carmela for wood-fired paella (book ahead)
- **Central Market area** - Great for cheap breakfast/picnic supplies (20-40% below supermarket prices)
- **Avoid** eating right next to City of Arts and Sciences - higher prices, lower quality

### Budget Food Tips
- **Main meal at lunch** (menu del dia) and lighter dinner = big savings
- **Central Market** for fresh fruit, bread, cheese, ham
- **100 Montaditos** chain: everything 1 EUR on Wednesdays & Sundays
- **Supermarkets** (Mercadona, Consum) for snacks and cheap wine (good bottles 3-5 EUR)
- Try **horchata and fartons** - traditional Valencian drink + pastry, ~3-4 EUR
- Try **Agua de Valencia** cocktail (OJ, cava, vodka, gin) at a local bar, not a tourist spot
- Tap water is safe but tastes mineral-heavy. Bottled water: 0.50-1 EUR for 1.5L at supermarkets

### Tipping
**Not obligatory in Spain.** Servers earn a livable wage.
- Casual meal: round up to nearest euro or nothing
- Nice restaurant: 5-10% if service was good
- 15-20% tips (US-style) would be seen as unusual

## SAFETY

Valencia is **one of Spain's safest large cities** - calmer than Barcelona or Madrid. Violent crime against tourists is very rare.

### Watch Out For
- **Pickpockets** in Central Market, Old Town, Plaza del Ayuntamiento, metro
- **Bracelet/flower scam** - someone puts it on you, demands payment. Say "No" firmly.
- **Fake petition scam** - distraction while accomplice pickpockets
- **Taxi overcharging** from airport - insist on the meter, fare should be ~20-25 EUR

### Tips
- Use a cross-body bag in crowded areas
- Leave passport in hotel safe, carry a photocopy
- Don't leave bags on chair backs in restaurants
- Emergency number: **112** (police, fire, ambulance)

## SPANISH MEAL TIMES

| Meal | Time | What |
|------|------|------|
| Desayuno (breakfast) | 7:30-10:00 | Coffee + toast or pastry |
| Almuerzo (snack) | 10:30-11:30 | Sandwich + beer (very Valencian!) |
| **Comida (lunch)** | **14:00-16:00** | **Main meal of the day** |
| Merienda (snack) | 17:00-18:30 | Coffee, pastry, or horchata |
| Cena (dinner) | 21:00-23:00 | Lighter than lunch |

**Key:** Don't try to eat dinner at 18:00-19:00 - restaurants will be closed between lunch and dinner. Enjoy a merienda (snack) instead and eat dinner after 21:00.

### Siesta (14:00-17:00)
Small shops may close in the afternoon. Supermarkets, tourist shops, museums, and major attractions stay open. Great time for parks, Turia Gardens, or a long lunch.

## MONEY-SAVING CHEAT SHEET

1. **Sunday = free museum day** (La Lonja, towers, IVAM, Ceramics Museum, all municipal museums)
2. **Menu del dia** at lunch (10-18 EUR for 3 courses!) instead of a la carte
3. **Walk or bike** - the city is flat and compact
4. **Valenbisi** weekly pass (13.30 EUR) with free 30-min rides
5. **Central Market** for cheap fresh food and picnic supplies
6. **Free walking tours** instead of paid ones (tip 5-10 EUR)
7. **Tourist Card bundle** with Oceanografic if visiting City of Arts and Sciences
8. **Bioparc "Biotarding"** promo code for 20% off afternoon entry
9. **100 Montaditos** on Wednesdays and Sundays (everything 1 EUR)
10. **Buy wine at supermarkets** (3-5 EUR for good bottles) for evening drinks

## LAS FALLAS NOTE

The famous Fallas festival runs March 15-19, ending with La Crema (the burning) on March 19-20. You arrive March 21 - **just after it ends**. You'll miss the festival itself but benefit from:
- Post-festival pricing (hotels/restaurants return to normal)
- Smaller crowds
- Spring energy in the city
- Visit the **Museo Fallero** (free on Sundays) to see preserved Fallas figures
