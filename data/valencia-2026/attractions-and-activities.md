## MUST-DO: OCEANOGRAFIC — Europe's Largest Aquarium
*Full day | City of Arts and Sciences*

Europe's biggest aquarium: 110,000 m², 45,000+ animals, 500+ species across 10 zones. Not a "look at some fish" place — this is a full-day immersive experience.

### The Zones

**Arctic Zone** — Home to the **only beluga whales in Europe**. This alone makes Oceanografic special. Walruses and cold-water species in climate-controlled habitats.

**Antarctic Zone** — Gentoo and Humboldt penguin colony with underwater viewing windows.

**Oceans (Atlantic/Pacific)** — The largest single tank. Bull sharks, grey reef sharks, sawfish, and the bizarre mola mola (ocean sunfish). Eagle rays glide overhead.

**Temperate & Tropical Seas** — Connected by the **longest underwater tunnel in Europe (70 metres)**. Walk beneath sharks and rays. Tropical coral reefs with clownfish, lionfish, moray eels.

**Dolphinarium** — One of the world's largest. ~30 bottlenose dolphins, 1,500-seat grandstand. The dolphin show (~45 min) is **included in your ticket** — educational, not circus-style. Arrive 20 min early for mid-centre seats (best view). Front rows = splash zone.

**Wetlands & Aviary** — A striking **26-metre glass sphere** recreating mangrove and Mediterranean marshland ecosystems. Walk-through aviary with ibis, spoonbills, herons.

**Mediterranean** — 9 aquariums with local species: moray eels, groupers, seahorses, octopus.

**Red Sea Auditorium** — Underwater seating surrounded by a coral reef aquarium.

**Islands** — Open-air Patagonian sea lion habitat.

**Access Building** — First impression: hammerhead sharks and a mesmerising jellyfish gallery.

## MUST-DO: BIOPARC — Immersive African Zoo
*Separate day, half-day (3.5-4 hrs) | Western edge of city, Cabecera Park*

100,000 m² of "zoo-immersion" — no visible cages, bars, or railings. Rivers, rocks, and vegetation serve as natural barriers. You feel like you're walking through Africa, not a zoo.

### The Habitats

**African Savanna** — The largest zone. African elephants (calves Makena and Malik still young and playful), Baringo giraffes, African lions on elevated rock "watchtowers," **baby white rhinoceros Kairu** (born Nov 2025, ~5 months old), zebras, meerkats, flamingos, gazelles.

**Equatorial Forest** — Dense tropical rainforest. Western lowland gorillas at close range through a primate interpretation centre. Chimpanzees (including infant Ekon). Leopards. Pygmy hippos. Red river hogs. Enter through a fallen tree trunk to reach the chimp forest.

**Kitum Cave & Wetlands** — Recreation of Kenya's Mount Elgon caves. **Spectacular underwater hippo viewing gallery** — Nile hippos swim past the glass. Nile crocodiles (3+ metres). From inside the cave, watch elephants lick mineral salts — mimicking real behaviour at the actual Kitum Cave.

**Madagascar Island** — Ring-tailed lemurs **roam freely along the pathways** and may approach you. Also ruffed lemurs, fossa (Madagascar's apex predator), and panther chameleons. The closest wildlife encounter in the park.

### Practical Info

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | ~29.50 EUR/adult online (included in combo package) |
| **Hours** | 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM (March) |
| **Time needed** | 3.5-4 hours with feedings and lunch |
| **Best time** | Arrive at 10:00 AM — animals most active in cool mornings |
| **Getting there** | Metro lines 3/5 to Nou d'Octubre + 10 min walk. Parking: 5 EUR |

### Pro Tips
- **Start with the savanna** — elephants and giraffes most active in the morning
- **Don't miss the underwater hippo window** — the park's highlight
- **Keeper talks** ~10:30, 13:30, 15:30 (~20 min). **Feedings** ~11:00, 14:00, 16:00 — gorillas, lemurs, meerkats are the popular ones
- You CAN bring food (plastic bottles only). There are picnic areas. Bioparc Cafe inside for quick meals
- **March is ideal**: 18-22°C, animals active, baby animals playful, lower crowds

### Why Late March is Perfect
- Baby rhino Kairu at 5 months — small and active
- Baby chimp Ekon reintegrated with family
- Spring vegetation makes habitats lush
- Comfortable temps — not the summer heat that makes animals lethargic
- Weekday mornings are quiet

## SCIENCE & QUIRKY MUSEUMS — The Fun Stuff

### Museu de les Ciencies (Science Museum)
*City of Arts and Sciences — pair with Hemisferic*

Three floors of genuinely interactive, hands-on science. Not dumbed down for kids — adults love this. The building itself (Calatrava's whale skeleton design) is worth seeing.

**Standout exhibits:**
- **Leonardo da Vinci Exhibition** — 50+ large-scale machines built from his sketches, 360° Mona Lisa replica, augmented reality. Worth the visit alone
- **"Mars: Conquering a Dream"** — space exploration
- **Chromosome Forest** — 100+ modules on human biology and genetics
- **ESA permanent exhibit** — life on the International Space Station
- **"Science on Stage"** — live participatory demonstrations

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | ~8 EUR (included in combo package) |
| **Time needed** | 2-3 hours (science enthusiasts: 4) |
| **Tip** | Go weekday morning to avoid school groups |

### L'Hemisferic (IMAX / Planetarium)
*City of Arts and Sciences*

Europe's largest IMAX screen (900 m²) inside a giant eye-shaped building. The reflection in the water pool makes it look like an eye opening — stunning at sunset. Shows rotate: nature docs, space films, planetarium sessions. **Planetarium shows are best for adults.** Headsets in English.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | ~7-9 EUR per show (included in combo package) |
| **Time needed** | 45 min - 1 hour |
| **Tip** | Check schedule at cac.es/en/hemisferic/ — pick a show that appeals to you |

### L'Iber — Museum of Toy Soldiers ★ Hidden Gem
*Old town, Palau de Malferit*

The **world's largest collection of historical miniatures**: ~100,000 figures on display (1 million+ in the full collection). 15 rooms in a historic palace with obsessively detailed dioramas — battles from Ancient Greece through WWII to science fiction. Even with zero interest in toy soldiers, the sheer scale and craftsmanship is jaw-dropping. Adults enjoy it more than kids.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | 8 EUR (5 EUR under 26) |
| **Hours** | Tue-Sat 11:00-14:00 & 16:00-19:00, Sun 11:00-14:00 |
| **Time needed** | 1-2 hours |

### L'Almoina Archaeological Centre ★ Underrated
*Underground, beneath Plaza de la Almoina next to the Cathedral*

2,500 m² of underground ruins spanning **2,000+ years**: Roman baths and roads from Valencia's founding (138 BC), Visigoth cathedral remains, Islamic fortifications. Walk through layered history beneath a modern plaza. Reviewers call it "worth ten times the price." One of Europe's most important urban archaeological sites — yet most tourists walk right over it.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | 2 EUR. **Free on Sundays** |
| **Hours** | Tue-Sat 10:00-19:00, Sun 10:00-14:00 |
| **Time needed** | 45 min - 1 hour |

### Museum of Illusions
*Central Valencia*

60+ exhibits: holograms, optical illusions, impossible rooms, gravity-defying perspectives. It's a franchise (exists in many cities) — if you've been to one elsewhere it's the same concept. Fun and photogenic for an hour, more playful than educational. Good lighthearted break.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | ~12-14 EUR |
| **Hours** | Daily 10:00-21:00 (running until Dec 2026) |
| **Time needed** | 45 min - 1 hour |

> **No Camera Obscura in Valencia** — nearest are in Cadiz and Seville. The Museum of Illusions is the closest equivalent for optical fun.

### Prehistoric Caves Immersive Exhibition ★ Ending Soon
*Museum of Prehistory — until March 31, 2026*

Recreates 200 metres of French prehistoric cave galleries with projections, hyper-realistic reconstructions, lighting effects, and natural sounds. Temporary exhibition ending right around your visit — check availability. Excellent for science-minded visitors.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | Free (Museum of Prehistory is always free) |
| **Time needed** | ~1 hour |

### Museo del Arroz (Rice Museum)
*Near the port*

Dedicated entirely to rice — cultivation, processing, and its central role in Valencian cuisine. This is paella's birthplace. Housed in a historic rice mill. Quick, cheap, charmingly specific. Pairs well with eating paella nearby.

| | |

| **Time needed** | 30-45 minutes |

## FALLAS — What You Can Still Experience

You arrive March 21, two days after the Crema (the burning). All 700+ monument sculptures are gone — Valencia's legendary cleanup crews clear the streets overnight. By March 21 you'd barely know the festival happened, aside from some scorch marks on the pavement. But the Fallas spirit lives on:

### Museo Fallero — The Pardoned Ninots
Every year the public votes to "pardon" one figure from the flames. This museum houses **every pardoned ninot since 1934** — nearly a century of Valencian artistry, satire, and craftsmanship. See the evolution from crude 1930s wooden dolls to today's hyper-detailed sculptures. Includes historical posters, photos, and a video explaining the tradition.

Located near City of Arts and Sciences — pair them together.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Price** | 2 EUR. **Free on Sundays** |
| **Hours** | Mon-Sat 10:00-19:00, Sun 10:00-14:00 |
| **Time needed** | 45 min - 1.5 hours |
| **Worth it?** | At 2 EUR, absolutely. Better post-festival when it's quiet |

### Hands-On Fallas Workshop ★ Recommended
Work alongside actual fallas craftsmen in the Ciutat Fallera (warehouse district where next year's monuments are already being planned). Carve, sand, and paint your own small ninot to take home.
- **ArtValencia** (artvalencia.com) — studio visit + hands-on workshop
- **Valencia&Go** (valenciaandgo.com) — "Fallas Festival All Year" experience
- **ExperienciasCV** — workshop with working fallas artists
- **GetYourGuide** — guided tour + workshop combo (~25-40 EUR/person)

### Museo del Gremio de Artistas Falleros
Second Fallas museum in Ciutat Fallera. Shows how fallas are actually built — tools, techniques, workshops. Mon-Fri 10:00-14:00 & 16:00-19:00, Sat 10:00-14:00.

## LANDMARKS & ARCHITECTURE

### Valencia Cathedral + Miguelete Bell Tower
Gothic/Baroque/Renaissance mix claiming to house the Holy Grail. Climb 207 steps for panoramic city views.
- **Price:** ~8 EUR (incl. audio guide), bell tower +2 EUR
- **Free during mass** (Mon-Sat 7:30-9:30 & 18:30-20:30, Sun 7:30-13:30 & 18:30-20:30)

### La Lonja de la Seda (Silk Exchange) — UNESCO
15th-century Gothic trading hall with stunning twisted columns. One of Europe's best-preserved medieval buildings.
- **Price:** 2 EUR. **Free on Sundays**

### Torres de Serranos
14th-century city gate. Great old town views from the top.
- **Price:** 2 EUR. **Free on Sundays**

### Torres de Quart
Western medieval gate with visible Napoleonic cannonball damage. Less crowded.
- **Price:** 2 EUR. **Free on Sundays**

### City of Arts and Sciences (Exterior)
Calatrava's futuristic complex. Walking the exterior is free and spectacular, especially at sunset reflected in the water pools.

### Plaza de la Virgen & Plaza del Ayuntamiento
The two main squares. Architecture, fountains, old town heart. Free.

## GALLERIES — Pick One or Two

### Museo de Bellas Artes — Always Free
Spain's #2 art gallery (El Greco, Goya, Velazquez). Next to Viveros Gardens. Worth a quick walk-through for the building and garden setting even if galleries aren't your thing.

## PARKS & NATURE

### Turia Gardens — FREE
Spain's largest urban park: 9 km in a drained riverbed connecting Bioparc to City of Arts and Sciences. Perfect for cycling or walking between attractions. Includes **Gulliver Park** (giant slide playground — fun even for adults).

### Jardin Botanico — 4 EUR
16th-century botanical garden. Tropical species, cacti, greenhouses. Peaceful retreat.

### Viveros Gardens — FREE
Large park with fountains next to Bellas Artes museum.

## MARKETS

### Mercado Central — FREE entry
One of Europe's largest and most beautiful food markets (Art Nouveau, 1928). 300+ stalls. **Go early (8-10am)**. Mon-Sat 7:30-15:00, closed Sundays. Great for cheap breakfast/picnic supplies.

### Mercado Colon — FREE entry
Restored Modernist building turned upscale food court. Try **horchata and fartons** here.

### Mercado de Ruzafa — FREE entry
Local market in trendy Ruzafa. Less touristy. Explore the street art and tapas bars nearby.

## FUN & GAMES — Not-Valencia-Specific Entertainment

For when you want a break from culture and just want to have fun. All open in late March.

### Best Picks for a Couple

**El Hachazo — Axe Throwing** ★ Great date activity
Throw axes at targets. No experience needed, instructor guides you. Includes a drink.
- **Price:** ~19 EUR/person (15 games + drink)
- **Book ahead** — popular on evenings/weekends

**Escape Rooms** — Valencia has an excellent scene
- **The X Door** — Award-winning, multiple themed rooms. ~20 EUR/person
- **The EXIT** — English-friendly, good puzzles. ~15-18 EUR/person
- **Cubick** — Budget option, solid reviews. ~12-15 EUR/person
- All work fine as a couple (2 players). Book online 1-2 days ahead

**Virtual Zone — VR Arena** (Ruzafa)
Largest VR arena in Europe. Free-roam multiplayer VR games — zombie survival, space battles, etc. Not your typical VR headset-in-a-booth experience.
- **Price:** ~20-30 EUR/person for a 1-hour session
- **Location:** Ruzafa neighbourhood — combine with tapas and street art

**Albufera Sunset Kayak** ★ Romantic
Paddle through the Albufera lagoon at sunset. Calm water, birdlife, rice paddies glowing in golden light. Guided tours available.
- **Price:** ~25-35 EUR/person
- **Book ahead** — sunset slots fill up

**Racing Dakart — Indoor Go-Karting**
Indoor electric kart track. Quick adrenaline hit.
- **Price:** ~24-30 EUR for a 10-min race + 3 EUR one-time licence
- Walk-in usually fine on weekdays

**Fun Jump — Trampoline Park** (Massanassa, ~15 min from centre)
Biggest trampoline park in Spain. Foam pits, dodgeball courts, ninja courses, slam dunk zones.
- **Price:** 18 EUR for 2 hours
- Grip socks required (~3 EUR if you don't have them)

### Chill / Budget Options

**La Bolera Campanar — Bowling**
Classic bowling alley with pool tables and ping pong too. Laid-back locals vibe.
- **Price:** ~5-8 EUR/game per person. Pool and ping pong extra ~3-5 EUR

**Cines Babel — Indie Cinema** ★ Hidden gem
Independent cinema showing films in original language with Spanish subtitles. Vintage charm, curated programme. Perfect for a rainy evening.
- **Price:** ~8 EUR/ticket
- Check programme at cinesbabel.com

**Climbing / Bouldering**
Several visitor-friendly gyms. No experience needed for bouldering (no ropes, lower walls, crash mats).
- **Natural Climb** or **El Bunker** — drop-in ~10-12 EUR, shoe rental ~3 EUR

**Olhops — Craft Beer Tasting** (Ruzafa)
Taproom with 20+ rotating taps of Spanish and international craft beer. Tasting flights available. Pair with tapas.
- **Price:** ~3-5 EUR/beer, tasting flight ~12-15 EUR
- Casual walk-in, no booking needed

### Worth Knowing But Pricier

**Paella Cooking Class** — Learn to make the real thing
- **Paella School Montero** — cheapest at ~50 EUR/person (central, hands-on)
- **Mi Paella en El Huerto** — best experience at ~69 EUR/person (in Albufera, cook over wood fire among rice paddies)
- Both include eating what you cook + drinks. Book a few days ahead

**Hammam / Arab Baths**
- **Hammam Fatirayan** — traditional Arab baths, from ~95 EUR/person (check Groupon for ~40 EUR deals)
- Includes hot/warm/cold pools, steam room, optional massage

**Catamaran Sailing** — Cruise along the coast
- ~30-50 EUR/person for a 2-3 hour trip
- Available from the marina. Book ahead

### Closed in March
- Water parks (Aquopolis, Aqua Natura) — open June-September only
- No indoor skydiving/wind tunnel in Valencia — nearest in Madrid/Barcelona

## SUNSET SPOTS — Quick Reference

> Valencia's coast faces **east**, so the sun doesn't set over the sea. For true sun-over-water, go to **Albufera** (faces west over the lake). City sunsets are still beautiful — golden light on buildings and reflections.
